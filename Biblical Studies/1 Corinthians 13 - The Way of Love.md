### Context

The Corinthian church is super messed up. The church was arguing over and divided a by bunch of things like leadership, lawsuits, food sacrifices, spiritual gifts. With the last chapter, we saw how they were using their gifts to compete with each other. Showing off, claiming superiority over one another.

### v.1-3

- Paul lists some impressive spiritual abilities: tongues, prophecy, deep knowledge, miracle-working faith.
- He then moves to extreme personal sacrifice: giving away all possessions and even dying for the faith.
- Despite how spiritual these actions appear, Paul says without love they amount to nothing and profit nothing.
- The issue is not the value of gifts or sacrifice themselves, but the motive behind them.

Even self-sacrifice can be self-centered (Matthew 6:2)

### v.4-7

Paul explains what love truly is by describing how it acts.

In Greek there are four kinds/words for love
- **Phileo** - love between close friends or brothers, brotherly love.
- **Eros** - romantic love.
- **Storge** - familial love between parents, children, and close family
- **Agape** - unconditional, sacrificial love that puts the needs of others before oneself

Paul is specifically describing agape love. This kind of love is not based on emotion or attraction, but on choice and action.

Paul spends just as much time explaining what love is not as what love is, and he personifies love by describing it as if it were a person.

What Love IS:
- Patient
- Kind
- Rejoices with truth
- Bears all things
- Believes all things
- Hopes all things
- Endures all things

What Love is NOT:
- Envious
- Boastful
- Arrogant
- Rude
- Self-seeking
- Easily angered
- Resentful
  
Each of these qualities reflects God’s character, revealing that agape love is rooted in who God is.

Agape love is not about how we feel, but how we choose to act toward others, even when it costs us.
### v.8-10

- Paul directly confronts the Corinthian obsession with spiritual gifts.
- Spiritual gifts like prophecy, tongues, and knowledge will one day stop. Love/Agape however, never fails or comes to an end.
- Spiritual gifts like prophecy, tongues, and knowledge are temporary. These gifts exist because our understanding of God is incomplete.

- “The perfect” refers to being fully with Christ
- "The partial" refers to our limited knowledge and understanding of God himself, the most gifted believers only know in part. 
- Spiritual gifts exist because no one sees the full picture yet (understands God completely or has a direct unhindered relationship with Christ).
- Gifts are tools God uses for a time, love is the lasting work God is producing. Even at our best, we are all partial until we are with Christ.

### v.11-12

- Spiritual gifts are appropriate for our present, incomplete state.
- Paul compares this stage of life to childhood—necessary, but not permanent.
- Maturity does not reject gifts, but it changes what we emphasize.
- Right now, our understanding of God is real but limited.
- We see God indirectly, like a blurred reflection in a mirror.
- One day, believers will see Christ clearly and directly.
- When we see Christ face to face, partial knowledge will be replaced by full understanding.

### v.13
- Faith, hope, and love work together to define the Christian life.
- Faith trusts God before we see Him.
- Hope looks forward to what God has promised.
- Love is the greatest because it never ends and reflects God’s nature.
